The Basics

Broad Strokes

Wikimedia vs. Wikipedia

I think one of the first things worth knowing about Wikipedia is that it's merely an instance of MediaWiki; one of more than 800. And while yes, it's the most ubiquitous, and likely the only one you may be interested in now, it's worth noting because you're not working with an enterprise devoted to a single platform. The singular mission for Wikimedia may be at odd with the mission of Wikipedia (though yes, probably doubtful). Put another way, you're setting out to interact with an installation of said platform. It's unclear who the stakeholders are of other installations and so it's unclear what motivates feature development, versioning pushes, breaking changes, etc. It's worth noting that while Wikipedia is one of the most trafficked and largest websites on the web, it's also largely publicly funded and staffed largely by volunteers. This all to say, my documentation could be out of date within a week and your scripts along with it. If they're important to you, it's worth keeping up with Wikipedia

Hard Truths

I don't know about you, but this was an assignment as part of my CS degree. But seriously, the reason I set out to make these docs is to simplify what I consider to be the real utility of Wikipedia's API; requesting content. However, if you were to "browse" the API's content, I think you'd find that I/we are in the minority thinking that the API should be used to request content. Much of the documentation covers how to make edits, review the metadata concerning edits, reviewing the frequency of edits by specific users, etc. Obviously, for the sake of maintaining a credible resource, these services are crucial, but they're "noise" for those of us looking to create with the available data.


There's another catch... Before I get into it, do me a favor and install a JSON viewer for your browser. I have literally zero preference what you use, (I use JSONView for Chrome), but trust me, it paints a picture worth a thousand words. Once you have it installed, do me a favor and ping flickr's API via your web browser's address bar:

https://api.flickr.com/services/feeds/photos_public.gne?jsoncallback=processJSON&tags=monkey,fun,clever,wildlife&tagmode=all&format=json

We'll get into the "why" & "what for" of the call momentarily, but what I want you to notice is the return structure (made especially clear by your shiny new JSON viewer).
